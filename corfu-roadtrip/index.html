9:00 – 10:15 AM
Paleokastritsa
Beach · Monastery · Best swim stop
▼

Six turquoise bays carved into dramatic cliffs — widely considered the most beautiful spot on Corfu. Get here early (you will, at 9 AM) before tour buses arrive. The water is absurdly clear. The 13th-century monastery perched above the main bay takes 10 min to walk up and is worth it for the views alone.

10:50 – 11:45 AM
Angelokastro Castle
Byzantine Castle · Best views on the island
▼

A 13th-century Byzantine fortress perched on a sheer sea cliff — one of the most dramatic historical sites in the Ionian. Never fell to Ottoman siege. The climb to the top is short (10 min) but steep; the views over the northwest coast are stunning in both directions. Entry ~€4. Allow 45 min.

12:20 PM
Sidari · Canal d'Amour
Beach · Rock formations · Legend
▼

The Canal d'Amour ("Channel of Love") is a narrow sea channel carved through sculpted sandstone cliffs. Legend: anyone who swims through it will find eternal love. The formations are legitimately otherworldly — golden rock walls rising out of clear water. Sidari itself is a busy resort town; park at the canal and skip the main strip.

1:00 PM
Cape Drastis
⭐ The highlight of Corfu · Northwest tip
▼

The single best sight on the island. Pale limestone formations that have been sculpted by the sea into dramatic headlands, arches, and stacks jutting into deep blue water. Park at the small dirt lot and walk 10 min down to the viewpoint. If conditions allow, there's a small beach at the base — the water color here is unreal. Every "best of Corfu" list puts this at #1.

Loggas Beach · Peroulades
Cliffside lunch · Sunset cliffs · Swim
▼

Loggas Beach sits at the foot of 50 m golden cliffs and is famous for sunset (which you'll miss — you board at 6 PM). But the cliffs and beach are spectacular at midday too, and the clifftop tavernas are among the best on the island. Ideal lunch stop. Walk down the stairs from the village, swim, eat at the clifftop restaurant above.

Kassiopi Harbor
Harbor village · Medieval fortress · Optional swim
▼

A beautiful northeastern harbor village with a medieval Byzantine fortress overlooking the sea. Much less touristy than Corfu Town. The harbor is lined with fishing boats and tavernas, and the short walk up to the fortress ruins gives you views across to Albania. Good place for a coffee or ice cream before the final drive south.

Honorable Mention — Didn't Make the Cut
Porto Timoni Beach · Afionas

Two back-to-back turquoise bays separated by a narrow strip of land — one of the most photographed spots in Corfu. The panoramic view from Afionas village above is stunning even before you hike down. It didn't make the route because the trail is 30–40 min each way on steep rocky terrain — too much on a day that ends with boarding a boat. Worth a dedicated half-day if you're ever back.

🚐 Road Trip Tips

  • Roads in the northwest hills are narrow and steep — the Talento van is big, leave extra time on mountain legs and go slow.
  • Park in the main lot at Paleokastritsa early — it fills up fast by 10 AM in summer.
  • Cape Drastis: small dirt lot, 10 min walk down. Worth it every time. Don't skip it.
  • Fuel policy is same to same — fill the tank before returning Jun 14.
  • Corfu roads use Google Maps well — keep it running for the mountain segments.
  • Nearest petrol on the way back: Gouvia town, just before the marina turn-off.
