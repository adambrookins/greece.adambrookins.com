For the Crew

Ionian Wind & Weather Tips

🌬️
The Meltemi is the thing to know. This NE/N summer wind dominates the Greek islands Jun–Sep. It usually builds from midday and peaks late afternoon. Morning departures are calmer — this is why the captain will want early starts.
💡
The Ionian is considered one of the more sheltered sailing areas in Greece — the islands buffer Atlantic swells. The Aegean to the east gets much stronger Meltemi. You picked a good spot.
  • ⏰Depart before 9 AM when possible. Wind is lighter in the morning. By 2–3 PM the Meltemi is often cooking.
  • 🌡️Bring more sun protection than you think. At sea, UV hits from above AND reflects off the water. June in the Ionian is relentless. Reapply constantly.
  • 🌊Don't assume a calm anchorage is always calm. Afternoon swell wraps around headlands. Ask the captain before swimming off the stern at anchor.
  • 🧊Stay hydrated. You won't feel how much you're sweating in the sea breeze. Drink water constantly. The ouzo doesn't count.
  • 📱Windy.com is your friend. Bookmark it. The ECMWF model (the one above) is considered the most accurate for the Mediterranean. Check it every morning.
  • 🌩️Thunderstorms are rare in the Ionian in June but not impossible. If you see large buildups over the mountains in the late afternoon, tell the captain.
